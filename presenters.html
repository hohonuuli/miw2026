Marine Imaging Workshop 2026

Advancing Global Ocean Observation

May 19–21, 2026
Gibraltar

Poster Printing

We are pleased to share details for poster printing ahead of the Marine Imaging Workshop.
We have arranged for a local Gibraltar-based company, Signtalk, to provide poster printing services. The cost will be £29 per poster. If you wish to use this service, please follow the steps below:
  • Email your poster in PDF format (highest quality) to: sales@signtalkgib.com
  • Signtalk will issue you an invoice directly.
  • Once payment has been received, your poster will be printed.
Please ensure that your poster is emailed by 8th May to allow sufficient time for printing.
The conference team will collect all printed posters from Signtalk and arrange the display in the Sunborn Borealis Room for the event.

Abstract Submission

Abstract submission is closed.
Please submit an abstract from the following options: Oral presentation, Poster presentation, or Interactive workshop using the template provided. Email your abstract (and any attachments) to SMES@unigib.edu.gi. Abstracts should not exceed 350 words and may include figures and diagrams.

Oral presentations

Format: talks will be allocated 12 minutes, with an additional 3 minutes for questions.

File formats
PowerPoint (.pptx) or PDF. Provide any supplementary figures as separate files if necessary.
If your talk contains multimedia, note this in your submission so AV requirements can be arranged.

Poster presentations

There will be two poster sessions during the conference. Posters will be placed in the coffee area for the duration of the conference.

Size & orientation
Posters should be formatted as A0 (either portrait or landscape).
Lightning talk
Each poster author will be given a short 3-minute lightning talk slot during the conference to present their poster.
Bring a printed poster to the conference; the organizing team will provide hanging space near the coffee area.

Interactive workshops

The purpose of interactive workshops is to allow hands-on experience or in-depth discussions related to tools, information, or techniques.

Date
Workshops will be held on 20 May 2026.
Duration & format
Each workshop will run for 1–2 hours. Sessions will typically be a demonstration followed by an interactive hands-on or discussion component.
What to include in the abstract
Brief learning objectives, required participant prerequisites (if any), software/hardware needs, and a short outline of the demonstration and interactive activities.
If your workshop requires computers, special software, or datasets, indicate these clearly in your submission so organisers can plan accordingly.

How to submit

  • Download and use the official abstract template: Download template (DOCX)
  • Complete the template, keeping the abstract ≤ 350 words. Figures may be embedded or attached separately.
  • Email your completed template and any figures to SMES@unigib.edu.gi with subject line: MIW2026 Abstract — [Oral|Poster|Workshop] — Your Last Name.
  • Include any AV or infrastructure needs in the body of the email.
✉ Email submission ⤓ Download template

Please ensure attachments are clearly named as Abstract_Lastname_Initial_preferredFormat (e.g., Abstract_Dissanayake_A_oral.docx).

Contact

For more information email SMES@unigib.edu.gi — include "MIW2026" in the subject for fastest handling.
